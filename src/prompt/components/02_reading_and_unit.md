# 02 — Paper Reading Guidance & Unit of Analysis

## Paper reading guidance

Prioritize the Abstract, Introduction, Methodology / Approach,
Case Study / Evaluation, Discussion, and Conclusion, because these sections
often contain purpose and use statements. Evidence may appear anywhere, so do
not ignore other sections if they describe why the model was created, how it
was used, or what function it served. Do not classify goals from background,
related work, reference lists, author metadata, headers, footers, or conversion
artifacts unless the authors clearly refer to their own model.

## Unit of analysis

The unit is the individual model presented in the paper. A paper may present
more than one distinct model (e.g., different domains, artifacts, or case
studies); classify each separately. If the paper presents a single model,
produce one analysis. If it presents multiple distinct models, produce one
analysis per model. If the paper discusses modeling in general without a
concrete model instance, set "no_codable_model" to true and leave "models"
empty.
